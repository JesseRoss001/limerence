Understanding Limerence

Limerence is an intense emotional state involving obsessive thoughts and fantasies about another person, often leading to emotional dependency and unrequited love. At Born Free Limerence Coaching, we specialize in helping individuals recognize and overcome limerence, guiding them towards healthier relationships and emotional freedom.

Our coaching services provide you with the tools to manage these overwhelming feelings, break free from unhealthy patterns, and build fulfilling relationships based on mutual respect and genuine connection.

Learn More About Limerence

Signs You May Be Experiencing Limerence

  • Constantly thinking about a person to the point of obsession
  • Idealizing the person and ignoring their flaws
  • Experiencing intense emotional highs and lows
  • Difficulty concentrating on other aspects of life
  • Strong desire for reciprocation of feelings

If you recognize these signs in yourself, our specialized coaching can help you navigate these feelings and regain control over your emotional well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is limerence?

Limerence is a state of intense romantic infatuation characterized by obsessive thoughts and a longing for reciprocation. It goes beyond a normal crush and can significantly impact your daily life and well-being.

How can coaching help me overcome limerence?

Our coaching provides you with strategies to manage obsessive thoughts, understand underlying causes, and develop healthier relationship patterns. We support you in building self-confidence and emotional resilience.

Is limerence the same as love addiction?

While they share similarities, limerence is specifically focused on an intense obsession with a particular person, whereas love addiction may involve patterns of dependency across multiple relationships.

Can I recover from limerence?

Yes, with the right support and strategies, you can overcome limerence. Our coaching is designed to help you regain control and move towards healthier, more fulfilling relationships.
